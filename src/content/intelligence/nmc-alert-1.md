## The 54-Month Rule: No More Shortcuts
The latest NMC circular explicitly states that any MBBS degree obtained abroad must have a minimum duration of 54 months. We have audited over 200 universities in Russia and Georgia, and here is what you need to know:

1. **Academic Continuity:** There should be no break between your clinical and non-clinical years.
2. **Medium of Instruction:** It MUST be 100% English. Bilingual courses are now officially a 'Red Notice' category.
3. **Clinical Exposure:** The university must have an attached hospital with a minimum bed capacity as per local government norms.

### Why Agents are Hiding the 'Internship Clause'
Most agents will tell you that you can do your internship in India. **This is a lie.** As per the new CRFM regulations, you must complete your entire clinical clerkship in the same country where you studied.

> "A student who fails to complete the internship in the host country will not be eligible to sit for the NEXT/FMGE exam in India." - Official Audit Insight.

## Financial Truths: The Hidden 'Year 6' Expense
While most budget sheets show expenses for 5 years, the 6th-year internship in countries like Georgia or Kazakhstan often comes with a separate 'Clerkship Fee'. 

- **Tuition Fee:** $5000 - $7000 per year.
- **Hidden Clerkship Fee:** $1500 (Often not mentioned in brochures).
- **Insurance & Visa:** $200 annually.
